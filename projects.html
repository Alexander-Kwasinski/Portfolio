    Micromouse

    Member of Electrical Subteam

    In Pitt's Robotics Automation Society (RAS), I am a team member working on the micromouse competition. Micromouse is a competition where teams build a mouse sized robot that can autonomously navigate a maze in the shortest possible time.

    My role in this team was to build and test the physical robot. I choose what compenents were best to complete our task, and I put all of those compenents together on a breadboard for testing. In the process I also had to communicate with the other subteams to ensure compatibility.

    Skills Utilized: Arduino, breadboarding, communication

    Drone Challenge (UAV) Competition

    My team placed first in a competition of around 40 teams in the state of Pennsylvania. The competition task was to build a fully functional drone with a programmable claw to pick up a payload. We then had to traverse an obsticle course with the payload.

    For this competition I learned how to use CAD software, 3D printing, laser cutting, and how to manage a budget (we could only use $500).

    Skills Utilized: CAD, 3D printing, budget management
